# Not Really Here Media / “Not Really Here Group” — source assessment (BBB internal)

This note is to help us decide how to treat statements sourced from `notreallyheremedia.com` in the Gorton & Denton microsite.

## Who they are (basic identity)

- They present as a **Greater Manchester hyperlocal publisher + radio group**, with brands including **Tameside Reporter**, **Glossop Chronicle**, **Oldham Reporter**, and **Tameside Radio** (as listed on their Public File).
- Their privacy policy identifies the “Company” as **Not Really Here Group Limited** and provides a physical address in Ashton-under-Lyne. (This is a meaningful legitimacy signal compared with anonymous political-content sites.)
  - Note: the same policy page also contains older/inconsistent naming (“Website refers to Not Really Here Media Group Limited”), which looks like legacy template text rather than a carefully maintained corporate disclosure.

## Ownership / continuity with “Quest Media Network”

- Prolific North reported (June 2024) that **Not Really Here Group** acquired the assets of **Quest Media Network** following an HMRC winding-up petition; the assets listed include **Tameside Reporter, Tameside Radio, the Glossop Chronicle, and Oldham Reporter**.
- The Gazette includes a notice connected to Quest Media Network’s insolvency that references re-using a prohibited name and states an intention to carry on the business under “NOT REALLY HERE GROUP LIMITED”.

## Regulation / accountability signals

- Their Public File page says **Tameside Radio** operates under an **Ofcom** licence and provides a public file accordingly. It also describes their news bulletins as using **Radio News Hub** for national/international news, with local updates “in partnership” with the Tameside Reporter.
- The same Public File page states “Not Really Here Media Group … is a member of the Independent Press Standards Organisation (IPSO) … [and] abide[s] by the Editors’ Code of Practice”, and provides complaint escalation details.

Important: IPSO membership is asserted on their own site (Public File). In practice, this is still a useful accountability signal (complaints route, claimed code), but it is not the same as us independently confirming their status via IPSO’s own “who we regulate” lists.

## How they source material (what we can observe)

Their by-election coverage appears to include a mix of:

1) **First-hand event reporting** with direct quotes (example: their Levenshulme hustings write-up is bylined and reads like an in-room account, with multiple attributed quotes).  
2) **Interview / statement write-ups** where quotes are plausibly taken from an interview or a party statement (often with image credit lines).
3) Some lines that look like **second-hand claims without a link** (e.g. formulations like “Reports state…”) where the reader cannot easily follow the chain to the underlying evidence.

## Trust / risk evaluation for our use-case

### What looks reasonably usable

- **Contemporaneous local reporting** of a public event that our larger outlets may not cover. This is often the only way to get *anything* on minor candidates.
- Their **institutional signals** (named editorial contact in Public File, physical address in policy docs, Ofcom public-file framing for radio, and an explicit complaints route) support treating them as a real publisher rather than a purely anonymous campaign blog.

### What we should treat cautiously

- **Quote accuracy**: for hustings or live events, their quotes are not a transcript unless we can locate an official recording. We should treat these as **“reported quotes”**.
- **“Reports state …”** style claims: unless they link to the underlying source (Electoral Commission guidance, a named spokesperson statement, a document, a court record, etc.), we shouldn’t treat the downstream claim as fully evidenced.

## Practical guidance for the microsite

1) Keep using them, but be explicit in our caption language:
   - Prefer: “Not Really Here Media reported X said…” (avoid implying BBB has a recording).
2) For high-stakes / contentious quotes:
   - Either (a) find a second source / primary source, or (b) move the block to `slot="further"` with a “reported quote” note.
3) For factual/technical/legal claims:
   - Prefer linking to primary materials (Electoral Commission guidance, council PDFs, official statements) rather than relying on their paraphrase.
