# Great British Energy (GBE)
[status: enacted] [lead: DESNZ/GBE] [start: 2025-05] [horizon: long]

**Intent (plain language):** Create a publicly owned clean‑power developer to speed up building strategic projects and bring in private investment. [^gbe-act-2025]

**Mechanism(s):** Primary legislation; a state‑owned company with an investment mandate; joint ventures (JVs); participation in Contracts for Difference (CfD) and partnerships. [^gbe-act-2025; ^desnz-gbe-2025]

**Key claims & evidence:**
- [impact-proven] [area: energy] [horizon: short] Company established with interim board/CEO; initial local solar programme launched. [^desnz-gbe-2025]
- [impact-proven] [area: energy] [horizon: short] Early public‑estate delivery: solar rollout announced for NHS sites to cut bills (multi‑site programme with a published expansion path). [^gbe-nhs-solar-2025]
- [impact-likely] [area: energy] [horizon: medium] Pipeline identified, subject to final investment decisions (FIDs) and grid connection slots. [^gbe-plan-2025]
- [unknown] Grid connection timings and supply chain constraints. [^ofgem-queue-2025]

**Costs & funding:** Initial capital envelope £8.3bn (public) to mobilise ~£30-60bn private; returns long-dated. [^gbe-founding]

**Distributional effects:** Regional jobs in manufacturing/construction; community energy participation. [^nwf-taskforce-2025]

**Risks & constraints:** [risk: delivery][risk: finance] Supply chain; planning and environmental consents; cost of capital. [^nao-2025]

**Timeline & milestones:** 2025: Royal Assent (RA) and stand‑up; 2025–26: first JV commitments; 2026+: scale‑up. [^desnz-gbe-2025]

**Outcome score:** +2 — Structural enabler with early delivery steps.

**Mechanism(s):** Up to £1bn in support; programme support to improve local/project capability; standardised delivery approaches and business models; coordination with grid/planning and national programmes. [^local-power-plan-2026; ^local-power-plan-press-2026]

**Key claims & evidence:**
- [impact-likely] [area: energy] [horizon: medium] Government frames the plan as a major scale-up of local/community energy, aiming to accelerate deployment and local participation. [^local-power-plan-press-2026]
- [unknown] Grid‑connection constraints and local delivery capacity could cap near‑term take‑up without parallel “system” reform. [^ofgem-queue-2025]

**Costs & funding:** Up to £1bn (programme envelope), with final allocation and instruments determining value-for-money. [^local-power-plan-press-2026]

**Distributional effects:** Potentially higher local acceptance and a clearer “share of benefits” for host communities; impacts depend on governance and who can participate. [^local-power-plan-2026]

**Risks & constraints:** [risk: delivery][risk: equity] Complex project finance and volunteer capacity; risk that better‑resourced areas capture disproportionate benefits unless access is designed in. [^local-power-plan-2026]

**Timeline & milestones:** Plan published Feb 2026; programme rollout and funding decisions staged through 2026+. [^local-power-plan-2026]

**Outcome score:** +1 — High‑potential local enabler if simplified and properly supported; impact depends on execution and grid access.
